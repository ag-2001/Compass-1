Building a Support System

Going through college can be difficult, so it is important to have a support system comprised of people that will encourage you to achieve your goals and boost your morale during stressful times. Seek out family, friends, teachers, and classmates that will support your college journey, and don’t be afraid to ask them for help when you need it. Even if they haven’t attended college themselves, you can ask them to remind you to finish applications, help you review for tests, and give life advice. It is also beneficial to find a mentor that has gone through college and understands your specific situation. So, reach out to a teacher, counselor or community member and ask them for guidance on what classes to take, how to balance family and school, scholarship opportunities, and discuss any other issues you may have.

You can also join clubs on campus and meet other students who can offer support and even relate to the struggles of a college student. Find groups of other first generation students and inquire about their methods of supporting themselves in college whether financially or emotionally.

Remember, you are never in this alone, and if you need extra support, contact us. We would be more than happy to offer advice or moral support to anyone who needs it.
