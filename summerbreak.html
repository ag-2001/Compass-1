How to Spend Your Summer Wisely

Since youâ€™ll have quite a bit of free time, summer break is a good time to plan out what you want to accomplish in the following year. Organizing a yearly plan can help you stay focused on your goals and ensure that you reach the certain milestones you put for yourself. It is also important to use your time wisely during your break. Summer is definitely time to relax and recharge, but using your excess time to improve yourself and learn new skills makes college recruiters view you as a well rounded and motivated individual. Here are some things you can do over summer break.

Here are a few examples of things you can do over summer break:
-Participate in a summer program
-Complete an internship
-Take a summer class
-Learn a new skill
-Volunteer at a local organization or charity
-Practice writing college essays
-Study for the SAT/ACT
-Apply for future scholarships

Note that many programs that seem prestigious might be very costly and maybe be tricking you into paying large sums of money. Look for programs that are not expensive and teach you new skills. If you want the program to stand out when you add it to your resume, try looking for competitive summer-long programs. Also, if you would like to take a class, community college classes are usually free of charge for high school students as long as you enroll to the college through your school.
