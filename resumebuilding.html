Resume Building

A resume is incredibly important when you are applying for a job, internship, or certain volunteering activities. It allows for your employer to understand your strengths, skills, and experiences in order to assess whether they should call you in for an interview or hire you on the spot. You should make sure that everything you list on your resume is correct and reflects your qualities as well as passions. There are a few things you must have on your resume and several ways you can be creative with it! Use this template to create a basic resume and adjust it as you like.

The first portion of your resume is simple! The top of your resume is where you will add your heading. Your heading should include your name in a large font, your email, phone number and your home address is optional.

The second portion is where you add a brief summary about yourself and what you intend on pursuing. It adds personality and character to your resume and allows for you to have a place to express yourself.

The third section of your resume is where you can begin adding your work experience. As for high school students, this can include leadership, volunteering, sports or more. Make sure to include the length of the time that you were involved in the activity or write present. Additionally, it is helpful to provide a short description of your role involved. This can include the activities that you managed, something you created, or events that you organized. Be sure to use the passive voice when explaining on your resume.

Lastly, you can add sections for skills and honors/awards. This is the perfect place to list honor roll achievements or awards achieved from competitions or tournaments.
